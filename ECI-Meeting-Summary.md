## Key Business Challenges Identified

### 1. **Proposal Management (HIGH PRIORITY)**
**Current State:**
- Ted personally writes 90% of proposals, spending significant time on what should be repetitive work
- Proposals stored manually in Word documents on server
- 90% of proposal content already exists in past work but takes hours to locate and rewrite
- Repetitive sections (safety write-ups, standard content) are manually recreated for each proposal
- Difficult to find historical data: "Which project had this? Where is this information?"

**Impact:**
- Major time drain on CEO-level resources
- Inconsistent pricing/fee data retrieval
- Lost institutional knowledge
- Manual process when searching for comparable project fees and timelines

**Opportunity:**
- AI-powered proposal manager that can:
  - Search historical proposal database
  - Aggregate past project data for costing and fees
  - Auto-generate cookie-cutter content customized by industry/customer type
  - Improve document design and formatting
  - Preserve "tribal knowledge" for succession planning
- Estimated cost of proposal manager hire: $60K-$80K + benefits
- AI alternative offers 24/7 availability, no fatigue, extensive training data

---

### 2. **Staffing Allocation & Resource Management**
**Current State:**
- Manual staffing allocation using Excel or "archaic charts"
- Weekly meetings to update resource allocation manually
- Difficult to answer: "What's our staffing allocation?" during proposal responses
- No real-time visibility into resource capacity across projects

**Ted's Quote:**
> "It's mom and pop versus being a little more refined in having a tool that does this."

**Opportunity:**
- Custom resource allocation tool (similar to consulting firm demo shown)
- Real-time dashboard showing staff allocation across projects
- Capacity planning for hiring forecasts
- Better visibility for multi-office coordination

---

### 3. **Data Aggregation & Business Intelligence**
**Current State:**
- Disparate systems without full integration:
  - **Procore** for project management (~$125K/year for limited users)
  - Separate financial software
  - Separate estimating software
- No unified dashboard for snapshot of all activities
- Manual processes for building projections and budget tracking
- Data manually converted to Excel tables for reporting
- Software expenses increasing 15-20% annually

**Ted's Quote:**
> "I can't just go to one place and then have like a dashboard that tells me everything that's going on."

**Opportunity:**
- Data aggregation layer across disparate systems via APIs
- Executive dashboard showing:
  - Budget vs. actual across all projects
  - Cash flow projections
  - Resource utilization trends
  - Project health snapshots
- Mobile/tablet access for on-the-go viewing
- Reduce reliance on manual Excel conversions

---

### 4. **Cost Tracking & Project Financials**
**Current State:**
- Building projections done manually
- Different people in different systems entering data
- Difficulty tracking which jobs are falling off when
- Manual lookup required: "How many months were we on that project?"
- Need to quickly assess: "For $1M-$5M projects, what were our average fees, average time?"

**Opportunity:**
- Historical project analytics dashboard
- Automated fee/cost analysis by project size ranges
- Guard rails on estimates to prevent missing labor costs or overcharging
- Quick comparison tools for similar past projects
